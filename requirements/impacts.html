1.4. Impacts

The positive potential impacts of CSTutor include:

  • Immediate feedback for students on both lecture material and quizzes. No need to wait for a grader to provide feedback.
  • Encourages experimentation via integrated execution environment .
  • 24 hour access to lesson material. Students set their own schedule and pace.
  • New material available as soon as writer wants it to be. No need to wait for traditional distribution channels (email, paper handouts)
  • Gives students better context before seeking in-person help
  • Provides help to students reluctant to seek in-person help due to fears of being marked as "slow"

The negative potential impacts of CSTutor include:

  • Possible decrease in interaction between Student and Teacher
  • Poor impression of online tutoring due to badly designed lessons
  • Potential Abuse by instructors who use the system exclusively so as to avoid dealing with students
  • Lesson material my be incomplete/inaccurate (mitigated by restricting write access to qualified persons only).
  • Computer Science Tutors won't be able to work as many hours, due to students exclusively using CSTutor
  • Possibility for "forked" lessons if two professors disagree about how to present a particular lesson
